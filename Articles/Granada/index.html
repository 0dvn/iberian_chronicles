Granada

Granada is a city in the south of Spain, at the foot of the Sierra Nevada mountains. It is smaller than Seville or Barcelona, but it has a lot to offer. And yes — in Granada, your drink comes with free food.

  • The Alhambra

    The Alhambra is a palace and fortress on a hill above the city. It was built by the Moors hundreds of years ago. The decoration inside is very detailed — the walls are covered in patterns and writing. You need to book your ticket online before you visit, sometimes weeks in advance. Do not leave it to the last day.

  • The Albaicín

    The Albaicín is the old Moorish neighbourhood on the hill opposite the Alhambra. The streets are steep and made of stone. At the top, there is a viewpoint called Mirador de San Nicolás. From there you can see the Alhambra with the snow-covered mountains behind it. It is one of the best views in Spain.

  • Free Tapas

    Granada is one of the few places in Spain where you get a free tapa with every drink you order. You do not choose what you get — the bar decides. We ordered drinks in four different bars and got four different tapas. It is a fun way to try local food without spending much.

  • Travel Tips

    Book the Alhambra as early as possible — tickets sell out fast. Wear comfortable shoes because the Albaicín has a lot of uphill walking. If you visit in winter, bring a jacket. Granada is colder than you might think for a southern city.

  • Final Thoughts

    Granada is different from other Spanish cities. It has a mix of cultures and histories that you can feel when you walk around. The Alhambra alone is worth the trip, but there is much more to see once you are there.
