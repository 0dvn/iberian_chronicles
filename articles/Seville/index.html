Seville

Seville is in the south of Spain, in the region of Andalusia. It is a warm city with a lot of history. We visited in spring and the orange trees were in bloom everywhere.

  • The Alcázar

    The Real Alcázar is a royal palace with beautiful gardens. It is one of the oldest palaces still in use in Europe. The tiles and the arches inside are very detailed. We spent almost two hours just walking around the gardens.

  • The Cathedral and La Giralda

    The Cathedral of Seville is the largest Gothic cathedral in the world. Next to it is La Giralda, a tall tower you can climb. There are no stairs inside — just a long ramp. From the top you can see the whole city.

  • Triana Neighbourhood

    Triana is on the other side of the river. It is a neighbourhood known for flamenco and ceramics. The streets are colourful and there are many small bars. We had tapas there every evening — the food was very good and not too expensive.

  • Travel Tips

    Seville gets very hot in summer. If you go in July or August, make sure you carry water and rest during the middle of the day. Spring and autumn are much better times to visit. Also, most museums are free on Sunday afternoons — it is worth checking before you go.

  • Final Thoughts

    Seville surprised us. We did not expect it to be so beautiful. The food, the history, and the atmosphere in the evenings made it one of our favourite stops in Spain.
