Toledo

Toledo is a small city about one hour from Madrid by train. It sits on a hill above a river and looks like something from a history book. We went for a day trip and it was one of the best days of our trip.

  • The Old City

    Almost the whole city centre is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. When you walk through the gates, the streets get very narrow and the buildings are very old. Toledo was once home to Christians, Muslims, and Jews all living together, and you can still see the influence of all three in the architecture.

  • The Cathedral

    Toledo Cathedral is one of the most impressive churches in Spain. It took more than 250 years to build. Inside, the ceilings are very high and there are paintings and sculptures everywhere. We spent about an hour inside and still did not see everything.

  • Swords and Souvenirs

    Toledo is famous for its steel. The city has been making swords and knives here for centuries. All around the old town there are shops selling everything from small pocket knives to full swords. We bought a small letter opener — it was a good souvenir and easy to carry home.

  • Travel Tips

    Toledo is very easy to visit as a day trip from Madrid. The train takes about 33 minutes and is very cheap. The city is quite small, so you can see most of it on foot in one day. Go early — by midday the tour groups arrive and the streets get crowded. The viewpoint across the river (Mirador del Valle) is best in the late afternoon when the light is good.

  • Final Thoughts

    If you are in Madrid and have a free day, go to Toledo. It is very different from the capital and gives you a good idea of how Spanish cities looked hundreds of years ago. One day is enough, but you will probably want more time.
