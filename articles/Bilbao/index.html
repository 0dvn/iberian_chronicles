Bilbao

Bilbao is in the Basque Country in the north of Spain. It rains more here than in the south, but the city is really worth a visit. It has great food, good museums, and a very relaxed feel.

  • The Guggenheim Museum

    The Guggenheim is the most famous building in Bilbao. It sits by the river and the outside is covered in titanium panels that change colour in the light. Even if you are not into art, the building itself is worth seeing. In front of it there is a giant flower sculpture and a big spider. We took a lot of photos here.

  • Casco Viejo – The Old Town

    The old town is a short walk from the Guggenheim. It has seven original streets called the Siete Calles. The buildings are old and the bars are busy. On weekend evenings everyone seems to be out here having pintxos and drinks.

  • Pintxos

    Pintxos are the Basque version of tapas. They are small pieces of bread with different toppings, usually held together with a toothpick. In Bilbao, the bars lay them all out on the counter and you just pick what you want. They are small but very tasty. We tried many different ones — some with fish, some with cheese, some with meat.

  • Travel Tips

    Bilbao is easy to walk around but the metro is also good. Bring a rain jacket — the weather in the north can change quickly. The best time for pintxos is around 7 to 9 in the evening when the bars are full and the food is fresh.

  • Final Thoughts

    Bilbao is not the first city people think of when they plan a trip to Spain, but it should be. The food is some of the best we have had, and the city has a lot of character. We recommend it to anyone who wants to see a different side of Spain.
