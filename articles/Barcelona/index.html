Barcelona

Barcelona is on the coast of Spain. It has a beach, old streets, and some of the most unusual buildings you will ever see. We had a great time there.

  • Sagrada Familia

    This church has been under construction for over 100 years and it is still not finished. It is very tall and very detailed. We went inside and the light through the stained glass windows was something we did not expect. Buy your tickets before you go — the queues outside are very long.

  • The Gothic Quarter

    This is the old part of Barcelona. The streets are narrow and the buildings are very old. It is easy to get lost, but that is part of the fun. There are small shops, bars, and squares hidden around every corner.

  • Barceloneta Beach

    The beach is very close to the city centre. We walked there from the Gothic Quarter in about 20 minutes. In summer it gets very busy, but early in the morning it is calm. We had breakfast at a small café near the sand.

  • Travel Tips

    Watch your bags in crowded areas, especially on Las Ramblas. The metro is the fastest way to move around the city. If you want to see Park Güell for free, you can walk around the outside without a ticket — only the main terrace costs money.

  • Final Thoughts

    Barcelona is a city that mixes the old and the new very well. There is always something to look at. We would go back just to eat more — the food in the markets is really good.
